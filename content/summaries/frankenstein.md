# Frankenstein Summary (Plot, Themes & Analysis)

**Mary Shelley · 6 min read · 1818**

A tragic story about ambition, creation, and the consequences of playing god.

---

## Frankenstein Plot Summary
Victor Frankenstein becomes obsessed with unlocking the secret of life. When he succeeds in creating a living being, he recoils in horror at what he’s done—setting off a chain of suffering for everyone involved.

---

## Frankenstein Plot Summary

The creature longs for acceptance but faces rejection everywhere he turns. As isolation turns to anger, both creator and creation spiral into revenge, forcing readers to question who the true monster really is.

---

## Key Themes in Frankenstein
- Responsibility and ambition  
- Isolation and rejection  
- Science without ethics  
- The need for companionship  
- Revenge and guilt
