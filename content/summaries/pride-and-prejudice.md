## Overview
Elizabeth Bennet is smart, sharp-tongued, and determined not to marry for convenience. When she meets the reserved and wealthy Mr. Darcy, their early interactions are full of misunderstanding—she reads him as arrogant, and he reads her as beneath his social circle.

## What happens (spoiler-light)
As Elizabeth’s family navigates social pressure, gossip, and marriage expectations, she learns that appearances can be deceptive. Darcy’s actions gradually reveal depth and integrity, while other charming figures prove unreliable.

## Key themes
- Pride vs. self-awareness  
- Prejudice and snap judgments  
- Class, money, and marriage markets  
- Reputation, family, and social consequences  

## Who should read it
If you like character-driven stories, clever dialogue, and slow-burn romance with real emotional growth, this is one of the best entry points into classics.
