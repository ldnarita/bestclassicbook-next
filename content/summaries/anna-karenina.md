# Anna Karenina Summary (Plot, Themes & Analysis)

Looking for a clear, fast **Anna Karenina** summary? This guide breaks down the plot, main themes, and key ideas of Unknown Author's classic in a spoiler-light, easy-to-read format.

## Anna Karenina Plot Summary
Anna Karenina, a married aristocrat, falls passionately in love with Count Vronsky, leading to scandal and social exile. As her relationship deteriorates under jealousy and societal pressure, she spirals into despair. In parallel, Levin, a landowner, struggles with faith, love, and the meaning of life, eventually finding peace in marriage and spiritual reflection.

## Anna Karenina Analysis
Tolstoy contrasts destructive passion with stable, moral love. The novel examines Russian society, family life, faith, and existential purpose. Anna’s tragedy reflects the crushing power of social judgment and inner turmoil.

## Key Themes in Anna Karenina
- Love and Passion vs. Stability – Anna’s destructive passion contrasts with Levin’s steady, moral love.
- Society and Hypocrisy – Russian aristocratic society harshly judges women more than men.
- Faith and Existential Meaning – Levin’s spiritual journey mirrors the search for life’s purpose.
- Family and Domestic Life – Marriage and family are examined in both healthy and destructive forms.
- Jealousy and Psychological Turmoil – Anna’s emotional instability drives her tragic fate.

## Why Anna Karenina Still Matters
It is considered one of the greatest novels ever written for its psychological depth and realism.
