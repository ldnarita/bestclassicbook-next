## Overview
Dorian Gray is admired for his beauty and innocence. Under the influence of a charismatic cynic, he begins to see life as a pursuit of sensation—where morality is optional.

## What happens (spoiler-light)
Dorian makes a wish that he stays young while his portrait ages in his place. As he descends into indulgence and cruelty, the painting becomes a private record of his inner decay.

## Key themes
- Vanity, image, and identity  
- Pleasure vs. responsibility  
- Corruption through influence  
- The hidden cost of “no consequences”  

## Who should read it
Great for readers who like short, sharp, eerie classics with memorable lines and moral tension.
